How bad it it?

Every year, millions of men, women, and children are trafficked in countries around the world, including the United States. It is estimated that human trafficking is a $32 billion per year industry, second only to drug trafficking as the most profitable form of transnational crime. Human trafficking, a global epidemic that knows no boundaries, demands our urgent attention. Behind the veil of darkness, millions of innocent lives are ensnared in this despicable trade, their freedoms mercilessly stolen away. Shockingly, it is estimated that over 40 million people are trapped in modern-day slavery worldwide. These individuals, predominantly women and children, become victims of forced labor, sexual exploitation, and other forms of abuse. Such a grave violation of human rights calls for collective action and unwavering determination to bring an end to this heinous crime. By raising awareness and understanding the magnitude of this problem, we can unite in the fight against human trafficking and restore dignity to those who have been stripped of it. Together, we can ensure that every human being is afforded the basic right to live a life free from exploitation and oppression.

Project Polaris

Founded in 2002, Polaris is named for the North Star, which people held in slavery in the United States used as a guide to navigate their way to freedom. Today we are filling in the roadmap for that journey and lighting the path ahead.

Operation Underground Railroad

We fight to protect children in our own backyard from trafficking and exploitation by providing cutting-edge, specialized tools for law enforcement.

UN Office of Drugs and Crime

Administered by the United Nations Office on Drugs and Crime (UNODC), the Trust Fund's mandate is to provide humanitarian, legal and financial aid to victims of trafficking in persons through established channels of assistance, including governmental, intergovernmental and non-governmental organisations.
